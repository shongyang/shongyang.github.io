The Power of Now

ProPlanner helps users see the future...

by planning for it, one day at a time

Many would think that a paper-based daily planner has lost its relevance in the digital day and age. Not for the enterprising CEO and Founder of ProPlanner, Farhad Haque — whose ventures include his own line of books sold on Amazon, and online courses on Udemy. He now has a vision, one of an organized Bangladesh.

“It’s the first productivity notebook in Bangladesh,” says Farhad, a serial man of business for whom one of his many hobbies happens to be collecting personal journals of all sizes and shapes from around the world. Just like how a philatelist is drawn to their stamps, Farhad has found an appreciation for the beauty and simplistic efficiency in every planner he owns.

Now, the collector has turned his attention to pioneering his own brand of planners to tackle a problem many couldn’t see… but let’s say it definitely was not imaginary.

Decluttering through ProPlanner

You see, Bangladesh has, like many parts of the globe, quietly been robbed of billions of Takas each year by lost productivity.

And that’s more Takas with each passing year, because procrastination is becoming much more effortless with cheap internet and the free, limitless supply of online content — all the more justifiable for you to unplug once in a while.

“Everyone is dependent on their phones, laptops, and digital devices in daily life. We at ProPlanner are emphasizing the importance of using paper, which invokes connection, and cognition. Daily writing and reflective thinking encourages productivity,” he adds.

“People here are not interested in using planners. Kids and parents, for example, don’t fully understand the benefits of planners. Our mission is to educate the common masses on using pen-and-paper to improve their productivity and declutter from the digital.”

ProPlanner for everyone

Farhad believes that the key to becoming productive begins with recognition and awareness, followed by the determination “make daily planning a practice”.

“A lot of people listen and read motivational books and blogs on how to improve productivity… ProPlanner is for people interested in [improving their productivity through positive changes in their lifestyle and getting organized] but do not have the tools for it. This personal planner presents the perfect solution for them.”

The domestic response for the product has been quite encouraging, and is increasingly popular with the crowds of all walks. “We have clients working in multinationals, professionals, gym instructors, students, university teachers, and kids using our planners. It’s heartening to note that parents have bought planners for their kids so they can take charge of productivity from a young age.”

Marketing @ ProPlanner 

“Here in Bangladesh, ProPlanner is available on different online platforms. We’ve co-branded with country leaders like Rokomari.com, which is a leading e-commerce website for books and notebooks.”

ProPlanner also maximizes its brand exposure through numerous platforms. “The business is currently taking orders on Facebook, Instagram, and Rokumari. We are also developing a website with checkout capabilities soon.”

Still, the checkout process for ProPlanner is smooth-running. “Most customers in Bangladesh prefer cash-on-delivery. They can visit our Facebook page, [private message] us their details, and we’ll ship.”

The ProPlanner’s customer support team also ensures that client orders and enquiries are handled swiftly and professionally.

Looking for a meaningful corporate gift, it turns out that ProPlanner provides bulk discounts! “We have a discount policy for bulk purchases as well. Moreover, we also provide customization options too depending on client requirements.”

ProPlanner keeps its fans coming back with occasional promotions to look out for, participating in promotional trends such as the Black Friday with good deals of its own.

ProPlanner Corporate Social Responsibility

ProPlanner has partnered with non-profit organizations to donate a portion of its sales proceeds towards social welfare. Its recent collaboration with a notable NGO has put food in the mouths of orphaned children.

The company also provides free productivity training to organizations as part of its mission, which as Farhad iterates, “is to educate people on improving the productivity of their daily lives”.

“In the near future, our next big project is to organize an awareness campaign on productivity, and how, by using the ProPlanner methodology, alleviates the negative impact of modern electronic devices on our health and mind.”

The anatomy of the ProPlanner

We’ve seen the latest version of the ProPlanner, and we like it a lot! It’s made up of a copyrighted 10-point formula that has:

  1. Vertical Timetable
  2. Date section
  3. “Top Priority” list
  4. Meals and Snacks diary
  5. Health and Fitness section
  6. Water intake log
  7. To-do list
  8. Thoughts and Reflections Summary
  9. Rating Scale
  10. Improvement commentary

Although it’s a daily planner, Farhad has included two other sections for users who prefer to plan on a weekly and monthly basis. Not bad!

The ProPlanner has a polished, professionally-designed look. The finish and printing is top-notch, with a high-grade paper to match. The wire binding also makes the journal very easy to flip. It arrives at your doorstep in a box adorned with its iconic colour and flourishes. The company is also one of the very few in Bangladesh (or anywhere in the world for that matter) dedicated enough to product quality to have a 7-day Return Policy.
